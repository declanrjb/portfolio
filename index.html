About Me

There's a point, around age twenty, when you have to choose whether to be like everybody else the rest of your life, or to make a virtue of your peculiarities.

Those who build walls are their own prisoners. ... I'm going to go unbuild walls.”

— Ursula K. Le Guin, The Dispossessed

In Ursula Le Guin's 1974 novel The Dispossessed, the protagonist Shevek struggles to understand his place as he's torn between two worlds. It's a story I've identified with ever since I first read the book in highschool. As a computer scientist with skill in mathematics and data science, I saw no clear way to pursue a love of writing. And as a writer and passionate student journalist, I struggled to see the value of my skills in computer science.

When I first learned about the field of data journalism, however, I felt an instant sense of connection. As an editor of my college paper, the Quest, I've made it my mission to "unbuild walls" in journalism, leading a series of investigative stories driven by data.

In the summer of 2022, I drove to D.C. to attend the Associated Collegiate Press's first ever digital journalism workshop, where I worked directly with experts in the field, including Washington Post Engineering Director Jeremy Bowers and New York Times Graphics Editor Larry Buchanan, on a project of my own design.

In spring 2023, I obtained a grant to attend ACP's spring college journalism conference in San Francisco, where I was accepted to give talks on both mobile app design and webscraping for data journalism.

And during the summer of 2023, I enrolled in the Data Visualization Society mentorship program, where I was lucky enough to be mentored by Reuters graphics editor Julia Wolfe. Under Julia's expert mentorship, I improved my design skills in data visualization and developed a Javascript-based web visualization from scratch.
